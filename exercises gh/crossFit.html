CROSS FIT

A high-intensity interval training, that improves strength and conditioning by performing functional movements with explosiveness.

Burpee

A burpee is a full-body exercise that involves a combination of movements including a squat, push-up, and jump.

To perform a burpee, start in a standing position, squat down with hands in front and then kick your feet back to get into a push-up position and perform a push-up. Then, jump your feet back towards your hands and jump up explosively with your arms extended overhead. This counts as one repetition.



Push Up

A push-up is a bodyweight exercise that works the muscles of the chest, shoulders, triceps, and core.

To perform a push-up, start in a plank position with hands shoulder-width apart, and toes supporting your lower body. Then, lower your body to the floor while keeping a straight body, and continue until your chest is near the ground. Push up to starting position by straightening your arms.



Box Jump

Box jumps are used in training programs to develop explosive power, leg strength, and overall athleticism.

A box jump is a plyometric exercise that involves jumping onto a box or platform from a standing position. The exercise typically starts with the individual standing in front of the box with feet shoulder-width apart, then jumping up onto the box and landing with both feet at the same time.



The Clean

The clean is an excellent exercise for building strength in the legs, hips, core, and improving athleticism.

To perform a clean, begin with barbell on the ground then grip the bar with both hands, keeping a straight back and feet shoulder-width apart. Then, explosively lift the barbell off the ground until the bar ends up on your shoulders in a front rack position, with elbows pointing forward and hands gripping the bar.
