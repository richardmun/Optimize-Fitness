Protein

Protein is an essential macronutrient dedicated towards the growth and repair of muscle tissue.

Proteins consist of amino acids which supports muscle protein synthesis. It provides fullness when an adequate amount is consumed. Protein can be converted to glucose when needed and can be consumed from poultry, beef, fish, and plant-based products.



Carbohydrates

Carbohydrates, like protein, are a crucial macronutrient vital for sustaining bodily functions and providing energy.

Carbohydrates are composed of sugars, starches, and fibers, serving as the body's primary source of energy. By fueling various bodily activities, including physical exertion and cognitive functions. Carbohydrates can be found in wheat, rice, oats, and other whole foods.



Fats

Fats provide long-term energy storage, and facilitate the absorption of vitamins.

Fats provide hormonal regulation including structure and function to cell membranes. Also, they are associated with reducing risk of heart disease and improved cholesterol levels. Fats can be found in nuts, avocados, oils, fatty fish, and dairy products. Excess fats can cause weight gain which should be consumed in moderation.
