NUTRITIONAL NEEDS

Physical fitness is a state of health and well-being. The ability to perform aspects of sports, occupations and daily activities. Physical fitness is generally achieved through proper nutrition, moderate-vigorous physical exercise, and sufficient rest.

Protein

Muscle Building Nutrient

Chicken

Lean protein essentially consumed for muscle growth, repair and weight management. Provides amino acids needed for building and repairing muscle tissue.

Beef

Rich source of protein while providing essential amino acids necessary for muscle growth, repair, and health. Contains nutrients such as Vitamin B, Iron, Zinc, Phosphorus, and Creatine.

Fish

High protein content in various types of fish such as Salmon, Tilapia, and Cod. Certain fish can be lean or fatty with both containing many nutrients such as Omega-3s, Vitamin D, Iodine, and Selenium.

Turkey

Lean source of protein that can be consumed in various ways. Turkey breast contains low fat calories which supports weight management. Ground turkey is another method of cooking for a low carbohydrate meal.



Carbohydrates

Primary Energy Nutrient

Rice

Carbohydrate source that provides a readily source of energy and replinishes glycogen in the muscle. Low-fat food source that allows individuals to maintain caloric intake.

Oatmeal

Complex carbohydrate rich in fiber which fuels level of energy. Dietary fiber aids in digestion which helps control appetite. Contains nutrients such as Vitamin B, Iron, Magnesium, and contains low sugar.

Potatoes

Complex carbohydrate in the form of starch. Provides nutrients such as Vitamin C, B6, Potassium, and fiber. Low in fat cholesterol which promotes a healthy heart. Contains HGI which can cause increase in blood sugar levels.

Something

Effective bodyweight exercise that utilizes the upper body, such as back and arms, to develop strength and muscles.



Fats

Overall Health Necessity

Eggs

High-quality protein with amino acids that aid in muscle growth. Contains nutrients such as Vitamin D, B12, and omega-3 fatty acids and is a low-calorie food that helps with weight management.

Oils

Oils such as Olive, Coconut, Avocado are considered healthy fats. Each kind of oil provides benefits in hormone production, vitamins and can be consumed for ketosis diet.

Avocado

A healthy fat that promotes heart and digestion health. Contains high fiber that aids in weight management and potassium which supports muscle and nerve function.

Nuts

Plant-based protein that provides amino acids necessary for muscle development. High fiber aids digestion which helps with weight management and include antioxidants which reduces risk of chronic diseases.
